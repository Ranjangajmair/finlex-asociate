Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in Nepal | FDI in Nepal 2024

FinLex Associates

FDI in Nepal is governed by the Foreign Investment and Technology Transfer Act (FITTA) 2019. In this perspective, the procedures entail initial endorsement from the DOI (Department of Industries) or IBN (Investment Board of Nepal) and final approval from the Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB). This summary will henceforth provide requisite information on the procedure of FDI, the requirements, regulations, approvals, and time needed for FDI in Nepal.


1) Main Laws Controlling Foreign Investment in Nepal


The laws that generally govern foreign investment in Nepal are as follows:


Foreign Investment and Technology Transfer Act, 2019 (FITTA) - The basic principle guiding FDI in Nepal, covering such areas as technology transfer and joint ventures.


Foreign Investment and Technology Transfer Regulations, 2021 (Foreign Investment Regulations)  - These were designed to complement and support the FITTA with more information.


Industrial Enterprises Act, 2020 - This is supposed to promote and regulate industrial enterprises in Nepal, giving certain incentives to domestic and foreign investors.


Companies Act, 2006 - Regulates the establishment and operation of companies in Nepal, including requirements for foreign companies.


Public-Private Partnership and Investment Act, 2019 (PPPIA) - Encourages foreign private sector investment, especially in the infrastructure, construction, and service sectors.


2) Main Authorities for Approvals and Regulation of FDI


Approvals for FDI and its regulation in Nepal are dealt with by:


Authority

Approval Process

Department of Industries (DOI)

For foreign investments below NPR 6 billion

Investment Board of Nepal (IBN)

For investments over NPR 6 billion or hydropower projects with over 200 MW capacity

Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB)

Provides final approval for bringing and recording foreign funds


3) Requirements for FDI Eligibility


For the approval of foreign investment, the proposed business must fulfill two requirements:


Negative List Check - Shall not fall within the "Negative List" of industries for foreign investment in FITTA's schedule.

Industry Status - The business should constitute an "industry" as per the Industrial Enterprise Act.


4) Sectors for Restricted Foreign Investment (FDI Negative List)


FDI is restricted in areas like:

  • Primary agriculture activities including poultry, fish farming, beekeeping and dairy.

  • Small and cottage industries

  • Personal services including hairdressing, tailoring, driving

  • Production of Arms, ammunitions, nuclear materials.

  • Real estate business (excluding construction industries), retail business, internal courier service, local catering service, moneychanger, remittance service, Travel agency, guide involved in tourism, trekking and mountaineering guide, rural tourism including homestay

  • Business of mass communication media (newspaper, radio, television and online news) and motion picture of national language

  • Management, account, engineering, legal consultancy service and language training, music training, computer training, and 8. 

  • Consultancy services exceeding 51 percent foreign investment


5) Stepwise FDI Process in Nepal


The main steps for establishing and operating FDI are as follows:

Investment Approval - Investment approval from DOI or IBN, depending on the amount of investment

Company Incorporation - The company incorporation with the Office of the Company Registrar

Tax Registration - The tax registration shall be done at the Inland Revenue Office

Local Business Registration - The company has to be registered with the Local Ward Office. 

Industry Registration - Industry registration shall be done with DOI. 

Non-Blacklist Certificate - Certificate to be obtained from Credit Information Bureau

NRB Approval - Approval from NRB for remitting the investment amount 

Infuse Investment Funds - Transfer of investment funds into a local bank account and get an Investment Certificate

Filing with NRB – File the invested capital amount with NRB


6) Minimum Investment Threshold


The minimum capital necessary to be granted foreign investment approval is NPR 20 million (approximately USD 155,000).


7) Documents to be filed for getting Approval


Following are the documents required for FDI approval:


  • Project report on project background, market, technical, and financial aspects

  • Bio-data or profile of the foreign investor

  • Copy of company registration certificates

  • Corporate resolution authorizing investment in Nepal.

  • Bank-issued certificate of financial credibility from the investor's home country.

  • Proof of source of investment and investment schedule

  • Power of Attorney to an authorized person to complete FDI registration

  • Passport copies for the foreign investor and the directors

  • Commitment letter to retain investment for at least one year

  • Joint Venture Agreement, if applicable


8) Approximate Timeline for Approvals


The approval process of FDI in Nepal takes more or less 1 to 2 months for completion.


9) Additional Approvals by Sector


Some areas require post-company incorporation licenses or approvals, such as:


  • Insurance Board for Insurance Businesses

  • NRB for banks and financial institutions

  • Department of Electricity Development in cases of energy projects,

  • Department of Tourism regarding tourism-related businesses,

  • Department of Food Technology and Quality Control for food industries.


10) Costs and Fees


Some of the major costs to be considered while doing FDI in Nepal include:


Foreign Investment Approval - No cost deposit refundable upon approval

Company Registration - As per the Companies Act

Industry Registration – No cost

Capital Currency Exchange – No fee for foreign currency issuance

Visa Fees – Accordingly Immigration Act of Nepal
